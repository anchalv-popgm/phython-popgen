# Keller & Miller (2006)

**Paper:** Keller, M.C. & Miller, G. (2006). Resolving the paradox of common, 
harmful, heritable mental disorders: Which evolutionary genetic models work best?
Behavioral and Brain Sciences, 29(4), 385-404.

## What this paper is about

Keller and Miller ask a simple but puzzling question: if mental disorders like 
schizophrenia and depression reduce fitness, why are the genes for them still 
common in the population? They test several evolutionary models against the data.

Key concept: **inbreeding depression** — traits controlled by many recessive 
alleles get worse when relatives mate. The paper shows fitness-related traits 
(height, cognition) show negative correlations with inbreeding coefficient (f), 
while psychiatric disorders show positive correlations.
